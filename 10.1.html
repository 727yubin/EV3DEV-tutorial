10. Debugging and Common Errors

SyntaxError: invalid syntax

This is probably the error that you will see most often. This means you made a typo(spelling mistake, not putting a colon after an if, while, or for, etc.)

IndentationError: unexpected indent

This means that you indented where you don’t need to.

IndentationError: unindent does not match any outer indentation level

This means that you didn't indent where you have to, or you didn't match the indentation(4 spaces).

NameError: name '<something>' is not defined

This usually comes up if you haven’t imported a module, or you misspelled the name of a variable.

TypeError usually comes up if you are trying to do an operation between 2 different things, like add a str and an int.

ZeroDivisionError means you’re trying to divide by zero.

In your programs, you can raise an error by doing something like raise SyntaxError(“Your message here”).
